A crooked case.

I have recently taken up residence on Baker street. My roommate is a peculiar enigma, and seldom home, perpetually absconding in his M3.

On the telephone, his speech was hurried. “This is Mr. Hoover, I presume?... Have one of your men replace my reflectors... The newer C model will suffice… Yes, leave the plugboard empty… I believe we have at last the positions of the infamous ‘Rotor Ruffians’. One is concealed on 13th Street. What a sissy… Intersecting with 15th Ring Drive you say?... Another on 20th Street. This man I will never, never give up… I see, 8th Ring… The last one is on 22nd, though he knows we are close behind and is fleeing along Ring Drive... Do not fret, this is not your fault, David.”

He left the telephone hanging as he pushed past our front door. “By Jove”, I exclaimed, “where are you off to?” “All in due time my dear fellow. In the meanwhile, open the safe, will you?” Surely enough, in the corner a large metal box sat, with six stars blinking back at me.
