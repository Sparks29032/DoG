A crooked case.

I have recently taken up residence on Baker street. My roommate is a peculiar enigma, and seldom home, perpetually absconding in his M3.

The whole of this weekend, though, he had remained confined to his study until this morning, when he rushed out and placed in my hand an unintelligible note as he scurried toward our landline. On it was, written:

On the telephone, his speech was hurried. “Mr. Hoover, I presume?... Have one of your men replace my reflectors... The newer C model shall suffice, but leave the plugboard empty… I believe we have at last the positions of the infamous ‘Rotor Ruffians’. One is concealed on 13th Street… Intersecting with 15th Ring Drive you say?... Take caution. A single misstep and it’s good-bye, my friend… Another on 20th Street… I see, 9th Ring… This man I will never, never give up… The last on 22nd knows we are close behind so is fleeing up Ring Drive... Do not fret, this was not your fault, David!”

He left the telephone hanging as he pushed past our front door. “By Jove”, I exclaimed, “where are you off to?” “All in due time my dear fellow. In the meanwhile, open the safe, will you?” Surely enough, in the corner a large metal box sat, with six stars blinking back at me.
